The Dexerto Logo

The Dexerto mark - aka. "shield" - has been imprinted within the collective conscious of esports and gaming fans across the globe. Our iconic shield has come to represent a tonal quality that reflects the very fandom we create content for - it's excitable, curious, opinionated and authoritative - purveyed by editorial across written and video mediums. The shield is our most important brand asset and should be used with reverence and care.

Dexerto champions esports & gaming's biggest moments and the personalities that shape them.

Rules

We have all seen basic branding rules. The same apply here.

Legibility is king.
  • Keep the shield...as a shield around the logo, to protect it from garbage.
  • Don't place dark logos on dark backgrounds...and vice versa. Unless it looks really cool.
  • Don't place the logo over images that cause blatant legibility issues.
  • Don't change the color of the logo outside of the light and dark variations.
    *Some exclusions may apply
  • Use common sense. No drop-shadows, no stroke and for god sake, no bevel.

Typography

Kanit and Lato is to be used in tandem for most applications. Using obvious hierarchical methods of bold and medium in headlines and regular and light to be used within body copy. Within video and specialty composites: Devant Pro can be used as a specialty font to add neccesary flare.

  • Kanit for headlines, subhead and body copy.
  • Don't stretch type horizontally or vertically.
  • Kerning and tracking to be used in moderation. Don't space too tightly or loosely.
  • Avoid the use of all caps for body copy.
  • Avoid using more than 3 type sizes per page.
  • Condensed font to be used with moderation. Mostly for video or avatars.
  • Maintain branded fonts throughout all works.

Color

Dexerto's core colors are yellow, dark and light grey. When in doubt, use these as your north star. However, vellow does not always contrast well on all elements. In these circumstances please substitute yellow and use our pink or teal tertiary colors. There are lighter and darker renditions for even trickier situations but those variants are to be used only if the secondary colors render legibility issues.

Dexerto also holds 5 category colors. These are used to differentiate between our Gaming #E0005E, Entertainment #038764, Esports#3D5AF1, Hardware #2C5D83 and Tv/Movies #7D1ED8 sections. These should be used primarily within these sections and used sparingly as the introduction of all of these colors within our main pallet could compete with the brand's established look and feel

Iconography

When icons are necessary it is important to keep things consistent. First off, keep the same level of stroke. We suggest 10pt and maintain that look with the set you are creating. It is understood that with slightly more complex icons, smaller or larger lines may be necessary, but just like with other elements of the brand, keep it simple, stupid (KISS). Varying rounding is also discouraged and to keep things consistent, aim around 10px rounding. Hard edges and rounded are ok to mix as you can see in the example provided.

  • Icons are seldom used within Dexerto. But that doesn’t mean there isn’t a time and place.Icons can be used in array of different mediums ranging from presentations, animations and even visually describing segments of our audience or departments. Above are some examples of icons we at Dexerto suggest when curating your own. Extra points if you incorporate the Dexerto logo into an icon.

Photography

Stock

Dark-Mode. Gritty, techy with a touch of neon. Evade cheesy cliches, or any stock imagery that may even add subtle negative connotations towards gaming. For example, someone playing a console with a messy room full of junk food, ignoring a partner while gaming or showcasing toxic lobby behavior. We are trying to showcase the positive elements of gaming. Headshots of people playing on both console and PC are preferable but due to licensing issues, obscure hardware is common. Compositing relevant frames over screens may be needed.

Editorial

Expression is key for CTA. Focusing on high-resolution images is obligatory for consistent quality within our editorial image production. Use your best judgement.

Texture

Textures and gradients to be used when appropriate and not overpower the main composite, avatar or advertisement. Maintaining a balance of negative space with minimal colors or abstract elements as tertiary components. As to not confuse, the textures provided here would need to be edited in a way that would render them more of an accent, rather than the focal point. These textures are often largely masked away to provide negative space for photography or copy. Opacity, blending-modes and delicate application is key here.

Editing

Editing throughout our channels needs a consistent level of care. This is by no means a rule, yet an example of how we could maintain a high quality look for our editorial and marketing promotions.

  1. Allocate high resolution image
  2. If necessary, crop element
  3. Add soft light noise layer, ranging from 15-40% transparency.
  4. Sharpen/color correct image. Add backdrop.
  5. Add effects to image with branded colors.
  6. Abstract and text treatment over the top.
